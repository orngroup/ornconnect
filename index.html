Strategic Rationale

Why this makes sense now

The ORN Group currently runs parallel contracting, transport, and cruise operations across multiple offices with duplicated cost bases and fragmented supplier relationships. By designating ORN Holidays as the group's dedicated UK & European DMC, the business captures four compounding advantages simultaneously: cost consolidation, commercial clarity, group revenue generation, and a scalable structure for outbound expansion.

01

Eliminate duplication

Centralise contracting, transport and cruise management currently spread across EU offices

02

Create a profit centre

ORN Holidays earns on every group transaction, not just end customers

03

Leverage existing funding

Redirect EU office budgets currently funding duplicated functions to the UK hub

04

Inbound first, outbound later

Build operational depth on inbound UK & Europe before extending to outbound markets

05

Scalable infrastructure

Systems, supplier contracts and team built for global group volume, not just UK sales

Core principle: ORN Holidays stops competing with TripFactory / other group offices and instead becomes the engine those offices depend on — for product, pricing, transport, cruise, and coach operations across the UK and Europe.
Core Service Lines

What ORN Holidays does for the group

Four distinct service lines, each generating income, each with a clear group mandate and a defined operational owner.

UK & European DMC — Central Contracting

Acts as the single commercial contracting authority for all hotel, ground, and experience product across the UK and Europe. All group offices purchase rates through ORN Holidays. Rates are loaded centrally by Ken Newton and Manish Suchde, and distributed via API or rate sheet to all sales offices.

Inbound · Group-wide

Global Cruise Management

ORN Holidays manages and operates cruising holidays for the entire group globally — coordinating the ORN Cruise transition (Skyjet → ORN Cruise led by Alia Taub), contracting cruise lines, managing pricing and allotments, and distributing cruise product to all group channels including TAG and TripFactory.

Owns and operates transport procurement for all ORN global offices. Partners with Adonis Iliopoulos for European ground transport. Manages the Skyjet vehicle fleet and airport transfer network. Global mandate · Alia Taub lead

Designs, manages, and delivers the ORN group's UK and European coach tour programme in partnership with Incoming Europe (Uma Somia, CEO). Phase 2 · Not initial scope
Revenue Model

How ORN Holidays generates income

ORN Holidays earns from the group through two primary mechanisms — a transaction fee applied to all rates loaded and distributed, and a revenue participation on all holidays sold or operated. This creates a recurring, volume-linked income stream independent of end-consumer margin.

Transaction Fee
Per booking

Group rate transaction fee

Applied to every booking made by a group office using ORN Holidays rates — hotels, transfers, tours, cruise allotments. Fixed per-booking or per-segment fee. Volume-linked so income scales automatically with group sales growth.

% Revenue Share
% of GTV

Holiday sold / operated participation

A percentage of gross travel value on every holiday operated by ORN Holidays — coach tours, cruise, transport — flows back as management income. Applies whether sold via TAG, TripFactory, or direct group channels.

Management Fee
Per office

Central contracting management fee

Each group office (USA, AU/NZ, Middle East) pays a periodic management fee for access to centrally contracted rates, the product hub, and technical infrastructure (API feeds, rate sheets). Equivalent to a DMC retainer.

Cost Recovery
Reallocation

European office budget reallocation

Existing European office budgets — currently funding parallel contracting, transport, and cruise functions — are consolidated into ORN Holidays' UK budget. No new net funding required; structural cost reallocation, not additional spend.

Cost Model

Transaction Flow

How a booking flows through the model

From a customer booking in any group office to product delivery in the UK or Europe — showing exactly where ORN Holidays generates income at each step.

Step 1

Group office sells

TripFactory B2C, TAG B2B, or group sales office takes booking for UK/Europe

Step 2

Request to ORN Holidays

Office accesses ORN Holidays rates via API, rate sheet, or the Global Product Hub

Step 3

Transaction fee applied

ORN Holidays applies its transaction fee to the booking — logged, invoiced to the office

Step 4

Product confirmed

Hotel / transfer / cruise / coach tour confirmed with supplier by ORN Holidays team

Step 5

Delivery & % share

Holiday operated — ORN Holidays earns % participation on gross value of operated product

Implementation Roadmap

Four phases from structure to scale

Phase 1 — Foundation

Months 1–3: Establish the DMC entity

  • Formally designate ORN Holidays as the group's UK & European DMC
  • Define internal service agreement and fee structure with all group offices
  • Consolidate European contracting budgets into UK allocation
  • Appoint Alia Taub as global transport & cruise lead under DMC
  • Confirm Incoming Europe partnership scope with Uma Somia
  • Migrate all group offices to central ORN Holidays rate access
Phase 2 — Operate Inbound

Months 3–9: Launch inbound services at scale

  • Launch UK & European coach tour programme (series departures)
  • Complete ORN Cruise transition from Skyjet — full product on-sale
  • Transport SLAs agreed and distributed to all group offices
  • Global Product Hub live — all offices accessing centralised rates
  • Transaction fee invoicing live with AU/NZ, USA, Middle East offices
  • First group management fee invoices issued
Phase 3 — Optimise & Report

Months 9–18: Measure, refine, grow

  • Zoho CRM live — full supplier and booking pipeline visibility
  • Quarterly group income reporting from DMC fees and % participation
  • Cost-saving audit vs pre-consolidation European spend baseline
  • Expand coach tour programme based on departure fill rates
  • Review transport pricing SLAs with Adonis Iliopoulos
  • Assess Middle East CEO appointment and regional growth
Phase 4 — Outbound

Months 18+: Launch UK & European outbound

  • Extend ORN Holidays scope to outbound FIT and GIT from UK/Europe
  • Leverage contracted supplier base for outbound product
  • TripFactory B2C becomes primary outbound distribution channel
  • MICE programme added as managed service for group
  • Explore additional long-haul partnerships (Macau, Cathay model)

Operating Principles

The rules that govern the model

I

Commercial authority stays in UK

All supplier contracts, rate approvals, and product decisions require ORN Holidays UK sign-off. India is fulfilment only.

II

One rate, one source

Every group office purchases UK/EU product from the same centrally contracted rate library. No parallel contracting by regional offices.

III

Inbound before outbound

Operational depth on inbound is built first. Outbound scope is added only once the inbound DMC operation is profitable and stable.

IV

Fee transparency

All transaction fees, % participations, and management fees are documented in a clear internal service agreement visible to all group offices.

V

No new funding at launch

The DMC is capitalised from reallocated European office budgets. Budget consolidation is the funding mechanism, not external investment.

VI

Tech-first infrastructure

The Global Product Hub, Zoho CRM, and API integrations are the commercial backbone. Manual rate distribution is a transition state, not a target.
