Strategic Rationale

Why this makes sense now

The ORN Group currently runs parallel contracting, transport, and cruise operations across multiple offices with duplicated cost bases and fragmented supplier relationships. Designating ORN Holidays as the group's dedicated UK & European DMC captures four compounding advantages simultaneously: cost consolidation, commercial clarity, group revenue generation, and a scalable structure for outbound expansion.

01

Eliminate duplication

Centralise contracting, transport and cruise currently spread across EU offices

02

Create a profit centre

ORN Holidays earns on every group transaction, not just end customers

03

Leverage existing funding

Redirect EU office budgets funding duplicated functions to the UK hub

04

Inbound first, outbound later

Build operational depth on inbound before extending to outbound markets

05

Scalable infrastructure

Systems and supplier contracts built for global group volume, not just UK sales

Core principle: ORN Holidays stops competing with TripFactory and other group offices — instead becoming the engine those offices depend on for product, pricing, transport, cruise, and coach operations across the UK and Europe.

Revenue Model

How ORN Holidays generates income

ORN Holidays earns from the group through two primary mechanisms — a transaction fee applied to all rates distributed, and a revenue participation on all holidays sold or operated. This creates a recurring, volume-linked income stream independent of end-consumer margin.

Transaction Fee
Per booking

Group rate transaction fee

Applied to every booking made by a group office using ORN Holidays rates — hotels, transfers, tours, cruise allotments. Volume-linked so income scales with group growth.

% Revenue Share
% of GTV

Operated holiday participation

A percentage of gross travel value on every holiday operated by ORN Holidays — coach tours, cruise, transport — flows back as management income via TAG, TripFactory, or direct channels.

Management Fee
Per office

Central contracting retainer

Each group office (USA, AU/NZ, Middle East) pays a periodic management fee for access to centrally contracted rates, the product hub, and API infrastructure. Equivalent to a DMC retainer.

Cost Recovery
Reallocation

Cost Model

Funding the DMC from existing group spend

No new external funding required at launch. The DMC model is funded by reallocating budgets currently deployed across European offices into a single UK-based hub. The net group cost position is neutral or better — while producing a far more capable, centralised operation.

Implementation Roadmap

Four phases from structure to scale

Phase 1 — Foundation

Months 1–3: Establish the DMC entity

  • Formally designate ORN Holidays as the group's UK & European DMC
  • Define internal service agreement and fee structure with all group offices
  • Consolidate European contracting budgets into UK allocation
  • Confirm Incoming Europe partnership scope with Eileen Corrales
  • Migrate all group offices to central ORN Holidays rate access
  • Hire UK CFO to manage European finance centralisation
Phase 2 — Operate Inbound

Months 3–9: Launch inbound services at scale

  • Launch UK & European coach tour programme (series departures)
  • Complete ORN Cruise transition from Skyjet — full product on-sale
  • Hire Cruise Manager, Reservations Manager, Product Manager, Ops Coordinator
  • Transport SLAs agreed and distributed to all group offices
  • Transaction fee invoicing live with AU/NZ, USA, Middle East offices
  • Global Product Hub live — all offices accessing centralised rates
Phase 3 — Optimise & Report

Months 9–18: Measure, refine, grow

  • Zoho CRM live — full supplier and booking pipeline visibility
  • Quarterly group income reporting from DMC fees and % participation
  • Cost-saving audit vs pre-consolidation European spend baseline
  • Expand coach tour programme based on departure fill rates
  • Review transport pricing SLAs with Adonis Iliopoulos
  • Middle East CEO appointment and regional growth assessment
Phase 4 — Outbound

Months 18+: Launch UK & European outbound

  • Extend ORN Holidays scope to outbound FIT and GIT from UK/Europe
  • Leverage contracted supplier base for outbound product
  • TripFactory B2C becomes primary outbound distribution channel
  • MICE programme added as managed service for group
  • Explore additional long-haul partnerships (Macau, Cathay model)

Operating Principles

The rules that govern the model

I

Commercial authority stays in UK

All supplier contracts, rate approvals, and product decisions require ORN Holidays UK sign-off. No commercial contracting authority outside 17 Hanover Square.

II

One rate, one source

Every group office purchases UK/EU product from the same centrally contracted rate library. No parallel contracting by regional offices.

III

Inbound before outbound

Operational depth on inbound is built first. Outbound scope is added only once the inbound DMC operation is profitable and stable.

IV

Fee transparency

All transaction fees, % participations, and management fees are documented in a clear internal service agreement visible to all group offices.

V

No new funding at launch

The DMC is capitalised from reallocated European office budgets. Budget consolidation is the funding mechanism, not external investment.

VI

Tech-first infrastructure

Zoho CRM, the Global Product Hub, and API integrations are the commercial backbone. Manual rate distribution is a transition state, not a target.
